Classification of brain tumors using convolutional neural networks

#datascience #machinelearning

In this work, deep learning models were implemented to classify brain tumors using MRI images. The study focused on evaluating the EfficientNet architecture, along with a simpler model, to compare their performances in terms of accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, and computational efficiency. The dataset used for this analysis contained MRI images classified into four categories: glioma, meningioma, pituitary tumor, and no tumor.

The results revealed that the EfficientNet model achieved an accuracy of over 98% across various validation folds, significantly outperforming the simpler model.

The performance metrics, including precision and recall, indicated high effectiveness in distinguishing between different tumor types. The use of advanced visualization techniques such as heatmaps and Grad-CAM provided valuable insights into which regions of the MRI images were most influential in the model's decision-making process.

However, it was noted that the EfficientNet model required significantly more computational resources and training time compared to the simpler model. Despite this, the trade-off between computational cost and performance was deemed justified, especially given the critical nature of medical image classification. The study emphasized that the cross-validation technique, while increasing training time by approximately 400%, was essential for ensuring robust model performance across the dataset
